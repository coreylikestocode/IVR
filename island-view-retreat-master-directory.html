??? Parks, Nature & Outdoor Activities

Bon Echo Provincial Park

16151 Hwy 41, Cloyne (15 min north)
One of Ontario's crown jewels. Home to Mazinaw Rock � a 100-metre cliff face bearing 260+ Indigenous pictographs painted in red ochre, the largest rock art site on the southern Canadian Shield. Designated a National Historic Site in 1982. Best viewed by canoe or kayak (rentals available). Trails range from the 1.5 km Cliff Top Trail (boat access required, three observation decks atop the rock) to the 17 km Abes and Essens Lake Trail. Swimming beaches on Mazinaw and Joeperry Lakes.
Provincial Park Kayaking Indigenous Pictographs Must-See Family Friendly
ontarioparks.ca/park/bonecho • Open May 8 � Oct 18 (camping), day use to Dec 13

North Frontenac Parklands � Big Gull Lake

Big Gull Lake, Crown Land
25 island campsites accessible only by watercraft on one of the most pristine natural environments in the province. The lake itself is 21 km long with 88 km of shoreline, 30+ islands, and sits on 2.5-billion-year-old Precambrian Canadian Shield. Carved by the last glaciers 14,000 years ago. Part of the Frontenac Arch UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.
Crown Land Island Camping Canoeing Free Access
northfrontenacparklands.com

Big Gull Lake � Fishing

Big Gull Lake (at your doorstep)
One of Ontario's premier warm-water fisheries. Species include walleye, smallmouth bass, largemouth bass, northern pike, perch, lake whitefish, and lake herring. Eastern basin (deeper, rocky) favours walleye and smallmouth; western basin (shallower) favours largemouth and pike. Max depth 26 m. Ontario FMZ 18 regulations apply � valid Ontario fishing licence required.
Fishing Walleye Bass Pike At Your Doorstep
Licence: ontario.ca/page/fishing

K&P Trail � Sharbot Lake Section

Sharbot Lake to Kingston (180 km total)
The historic Kingston & Pembroke Railway corridor ("The Kick and Push"), now a 180 km stone-dust rail trail connecting Kingston to Renfrew. The Sharbot Lake section passes through the heart of Canadian Shield country. Cycling, walking, horseback riding, ATV (where permitted). Flat, easy grade � former railway bed.

Zone 5: Ottawa & Gatineau

Canada's capital. Two hours from your cottage door. For European visitors, Ottawa is an easy same-day return � drive up in the morning, spend the day at Parliament Hill, world-class museums, the ByWard Market, a Rideau Canal cruise, and be back at the lake for sunset. Or make a night of it and add Gatineau Park, the Diefenbunker, and the craft brewery scene.

For European Guests: Ottawa is Canada's capital � equivalent to visiting London, Paris, or Berlin. The Rideau Canal (UNESCO World Heritage Site) runs through the heart of the city. A day trip from the cottage gives you wilderness in the morning and world-class culture by afternoon.

Gatineau Park

Gatineau, Quebec (minutes from downtown Ottawa)
Over 200 km of hiking trails just across the river from Ottawa. Popular trails: Pink Lake (2.3 km, stunning turquoise water), Waterfall and Lauriault (4.5 km), and Carbide Willson Ruins (industrial ruins in the forest). Three public swimming beaches with lifeguards: Meech Lake, Philippe Lake, and La P�che Lake. Non-motorized boat launches. Trail access is free.
