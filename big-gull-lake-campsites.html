Getting There

Two boat launches provide access. All sites require watercraft (canoe, kayak, or motorboat).

  • East: Helen Lane (1044 Helen Lane), off Ardoch Road near Coxvale
  • West: Thompson Road, off Big Gull Lake Road near Harlowe
  • No road permit required for Big Gull Lake
💰

Fees & Booking

Book online through the North Frontenac Parklands reservation system.

  • $25.00/night per site (max 6 people)
  • Includes one vehicle road access permit
  • Season: May 1 – October 31
  • Max consecutive stay: 21 nights
  • Bookings open November 1 each year
🌳

Campsite Rules

Follow these rules to help preserve the backcountry experience.

  • Fires only in designated fire pits or portable stoves
  • Keep fires small — use only fallen branches and twigs
  • Never cut standing trees or damage vegetation
  • Pack out all garbage — leave no trace
  • Use provided privies for waste
  • Quiet hours — no loud music or unruly behaviour
🐟

Fishing & Wildlife

Big Gull Lake is a bird watcher's paradise and excellent fishing destination.

  • Fish: Walleye, Northern Pike, Largemouth & Smallmouth Bass, Perch
  • Birds: Hawks, Ospreys, Loons, Great Blue Herons, Bald Eagles
  • Fishing zone: FMZ 18
  • Walleye slot size: 40-50 cm
  • Ontario fishing license required for adults
❌

Cancellation Policy

Flexible cancellations with reasonable notice.

  • 3+ days before: Full refund minus $3.00 admin fee
  • Within 3 days: 50% penalty + $3.00 admin fee
  • On/after date: No refund
  • Refunds processed to original credit card (3-5 business days)
📍

About the Lake

Big Gull Lake is located in the southern portion of North Frontenac Parklands.

  • Length: 21 km (13 miles)
  • Surface area: ~6,000 acres
  • Max depth: 85 ft (26 metres)
  • 30+ islands — most campsites are island-based
  • Spring-fed, crystal-clear Canadian Shield lake
  • ~2 hrs from Ottawa, ~3 hrs from Toronto
